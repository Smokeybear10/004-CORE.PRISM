02

High impact · Citations made physical

Bound Evidence — chart and citations breathe together

~2h build High demo impact

Right now annotated dots and evidence cards live in separate worlds. Wire them together: hover a card → the matching dot pulses gold; click a dot → the matching card highlights and scrolls into view. Bidirectional. This is the move that makes the audience say "oh, the citations are real" — without you having to say it.

Credibility · Desk-product UX

Keyboard Nav — they live in keyboards

~1h build Medium impact

Bridgewater desk users do not click. The presence of ? showing a shortcut overlay is a credibility signal in two seconds. / to focus search, j/k to jump events, t to cycle tabs, ? for the cheat sheet.

Why it lands

Bloomberg, FactSet, and Bridgewater's own internal desk all live in keyboard. The instant a partner sees ? in the corner, they know this was built by someone who thinks like them.

It's also five lines of vanilla JS — no library, no framework dependency. The cheapest credibility signal in the entire upgrade pass.

09

Function · Was it actually macro?

Macro Band — strip under the chart

~2h build Medium impact

A thin stacked-area strip below the existing price chart: VIX, 10Y, DXY, sector ETF return. Color-faint so it doesn't compete. Lets the user eyeball "was the move actually macro?" in one glance — the visual answer to the +Macro ablation. Sells the rigor of the framework without an extra click.

Visual answer: the macro strip barely moves on Feb 2. Whatever caused the cliff, it wasn't macro.

10

Above-and-beyond · Two products, one page

Story Mode — analyst desk by day, demo theatre on demand

~3h build High demo impact

A toggle in the masthead. Off = the desk you have. On = the page becomes a guided 90-second walk: chart pans to the move, evidence cards animate in one beat at a time, dimensions fill, the verdict drops last. Same data, two modes — one for the analyst, one for the audience. The single highest "above-and-beyond" perception in the upgrade pass.
