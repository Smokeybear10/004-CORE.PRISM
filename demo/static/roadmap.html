§ 01 · Tier 1

Quick wins.≤ 1 hour each

Six items, each well under an hour of focused work, each meaningfully improving how a judge reads the demo. If you have one afternoon, do all six.

01

Hover tooltip on flagged dots

What
Hover any flagged dot and see a four-line preview: date · return · dominant dimension · Lean / Fade / Skip.
Why
Right now the user must click to see anything. Hover preview lets a judge scan all 18 events in five seconds.
Effort
~30 min · Plotly hovertemplate + customdata
02

Color flagged dots by dominant dimension

What
Replace green/red dot color with the dominant-dimension color (red, orange, gold, blue, navy). Direction stays encoded by ▲ / ▼.
Why
Judge looks at the chart and immediately sees "AMD's 2022 selloff was macro-driven; the 2023 rally was demand-driven."
Effort
~30 min · marker color array
03

Top-citation strip under the verdict

What
Pull the single highest-weighted cited quote into a strip directly under the Lean. / Fade. word. One italic sentence with [chunk_id] after.
Why
Currently the user must scroll into dim cards or open the Evidence tab to see anything cited. Surface the strongest quote immediately.
Effort
~45 min · highest-weight dim's first citation
04

Cache hit vs live miss badge

What
Replace the static cached · attribution gate ✓ foot meta with a per-attribution badge: cache hit · 2 ms vs live · 712 ms · ~$0.78.
Why
Demonstrates the cost-control story — a key research-engineering claim around zero-spend live demos.
Effort
~30 min · expose timing header
05

Copy permalink button

What
Visible button next to the move navigator: copies prism.app/#AMD/2022-04-15 to clipboard. Hash already syncs; this just makes it discoverable.
Why
Judges share verdicts in writeups and Slack, not just by clicking around.
Effort
~10 min
06

Verdict-character pill

What
Small pill below the strat line: STRUCTURAL / TRANSIENT / MIXED / UNCLEAR. Already in the Attribution; not surfaced.
Why
Move character is the underlying classification; the verdict is its mapping. Showing both makes strategy comparison legible.
Effort
~15 min
§ 02 · Tier 2

Mid-effort, high payoff.1–3 hours

Five items that change the demo's center of gravity. The ablation diff strip alone is the single feature most likely to move a judge.

07

Ablation diff strip

What
Horizontal strip of seven mini-chips, one per ablation bundle: base_news → +sec → +earnings → +peer_news → +sector_news → +macro → +positioning. Each chip shows verdict + top-driving dimension under that information set.
Why
This is the additive-testing story the mentor called "demo gold." Right now toggling sources changes the live attribution but doesn't show the trajectory.
Effort
~2 hours · pre-baked ablations already in bundle
08

Coherence-pass diagnostic

What
Expandable panel under the dim cards: "Coherence audit" → which dimensions were dropped, kept, or rewritten by the Haiku second pass.
Why
Anti-hallucination is a non-trivial claim. Showing it visibly turns it from a footnote into a defensible feature.
Effort
~2 hours · expose attribution coherence metadata
09

Cross-ticker date scan

What
"Show this date across all tickers" — picks one calendar date (e.g. 2022-09-13, CPI day) and renders a 5-up grid of mini attributions, one per ticker.
Why
Shows the system isn't just per-ticker — it's a research surface for cross-sectional questions like "which names had the strongest macro signal on this CPI day?"
Effort
~3 hours · layout work + cross-ticker join
10

Sentiment baseline overlay

What
Thin gray dotted line on the chart: VADER-equivalent scalar sentiment over time. Marker color shows agreement / disagreement with PRISM's verdict.
Why
Mentor explicitly asked for sentiment as a baseline. The visual gap between sentiment and structured attribution is the strongest version of "we beat sentiment."
Effort
~2 hours · assumes sentiment_score available
11

Per-dimension time-series

What
Small chart at the bottom of the prism canvas: dimension weights over time as a stacked area. Click a date → focuses that move.
Why
Decomposition isn't just point-in-time; it evolves. Showing demand fade and macro spike during a recession is the kind of visual that wins prizes.
Effort
~3 hours
§ 03 · Tier 3

Stretch showpieces.half-day or more

Larger lifts. Each is a single feature judges will remember — but each costs half a day. Pick at most one for the next push.

12

Two-up move comparison

What
Pin a move as a "comparison anchor." Pick a second. Side-by-side prism canvases, evidence panels, verdict blocks. Shared y-axis so dimension weights compare directly.
Why
Why was Q1 earnings a structural beat but Q2 earnings a fade? PRISM should help judges answer that, not just classify each move in isolation.
Effort
~half day · significant layout work
13

Live "why the verdict changed" annotation

What
When the user toggles a source off, fade-in text under the verdict: "Removing 13F flipped Lean → Fade because the macro dimension lost 0.18 weight." A diff explainer.
Why
The ablation surface goes from "watch numbers move" to "watch the model reason out loud."
Effort
~half day · diff two attributions, template
14

Annotation / ground-truth capture

What
Small "I agree / I disagree / unsure" trio under each verdict. Saves to localStorage. Aggregate counts shown in a tiny badge on the eval strip.
Why
Lets a judge generate ground-truth cases by clicking through. Doubles as a feedback loop for the team.
Effort
~half day · pure frontend
15

Outlier callouts on the chart

What
Chart annotations like "biggest call" (model's largest correct contrarian fade) and "biggest miss" (largest wrong-direction). Curated, hand-labeled.
Why
Addresses the mentor's "include a failure case" ask explicitly. Makes the demo feel honest.
Effort
~3 hours · content + Plotly annotations
16

Print / PDF clean view

What
A ?print=1 URL flag that hides chrome and renders a one-page report: chart + prism + dim summaries + PnL. Print stylesheet collapses to 8.5×11.
Why
Judges who write up findings want a clean artifact to paste into a doc. Better than screenshots.
Effort
~3 hours · CSS print rules
§ 04 · Tier 4

Polish details.10–30 min each

Don't add core functionality but tighten the demo. Pick three or four for the day before the showcase.

  • Loading skeletons — replace the "—" placeholders with shimmer skeletons during fetch.
  • Smooth transitions between moves — cross-fade the dim cards instead of hard-swap.
  • Dim-card click → highlight evidence group — click any dimension panel to scroll-and-highlight its citations in the Evidence tab.
  • Sticky verdict mini — pin to top on scroll so the verdict stays visible while reading dim cards.
  • Compact mode (c key) — single keyboard shortcut collapses the chart card so the prism + PnL fit one viewport.
  • Ticker switcher search — type-ahead so adding more tickers later doesn't break the masthead.
  • Tooltip on dimension labels — hover "Demand" → see the persistence prior +0.85 and what it means.
  • Zero-state empty graphics — when no move is selected, show a faint Plate-I prism instead of "—".
  • Mobile pass — the page is desktop-first; one tight pass to make it usable at iPad width.
  • Sound on flip (optional, polarizing) — a single soft tone on Lean / Fade / Skip flip. Most judges will hate this.
§ 05 · Tier 5

Backend / infra reach.multi-day

Bigger lifts that change what the page can show. Each one extends the data surface, not the visual one.

  • More tickers — extend the pre-bake to 20–30 names so judges can stress-test the system on names they know.
  • Live attribution endpoint exposure — let users type in a ticker that isn't pre-baked. ~$0.75 / move on Opus.
  • Per-judge API key surface — let a judge plug in their own Anthropic key to attribute fresh moves without billing the team.
  • WebSocket live mode — stream the model's tool_use call token-by-token so the dim cards fill in real time. High demo wow.
  • Eval harness UI — clickable list of every ground-truth case with side-by-side expected vs. actual.
§ 06 · Decision principle

If picking three, do #1 + #2 + #7.

Hover preview, dimension-colored dots, and the ablation diff strip. Together they take the chart from "passive timeline" to "scannable research surface" and make the additive-testing story visual instead of buried in toggles.

If picking just one, #7 (ablation diff strip) is the single feature that turns the mentor's "demo gold" framing into something a judge sees in three seconds.

Recommended trio
#1 · #2 · #7
Hover tooltip · dimension-colored dots · ablation diff strip. Roughly four hours combined. Highest leverage on demo readability.
If only one
#7 · Ablation diff strip
Two hours. Turns the additive-testing pitch into a visual story a judge sees in three seconds.
