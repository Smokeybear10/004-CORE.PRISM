Advanced Micro Devices, March 2024: a structural-or-transient diagnosis

A research demo decomposing a single −23.82% price move across five qualitative dimensions, with cited evidence from filings, transcripts, and news.

NASDAQ:AMDSemiconductorsWindow: 2021-04-25 → 2026-04-2516 flagged moves

This page walks through one event end-to-end. We pick a single significant move (|return| beyond three standard deviations of trailing 30-day vol), pull the text we have for the days surrounding it, run an attribution model that decomposes the move across five fixed dimensions, and ask whether the move reflects a real, durable change in the business (structural) or an overreaction that should revert (transient). The output is a verdict — lean, fade, or skip — produced by four independent fade-or-follow strategies running on the same attribution.

The verdict

Finding 01

For the move on 2024-03-04 (−23.82%), three of four strategies converge on SKIP. The fourth, dimension-weighted, dissents toward lean because the dominant driver — demand — historically persists.

The model expected −18.00% from the text and the market printed −23.82%. The 5.82-point overshoot is small, the dominant dimension (demand at 0.45) reads structural, and yet the move includes a clear transient tail in the macro and competitive sub-stories. Three of four strategies treat the gap as too thin to fade and the character as too mixed to lean. The fourth strategy reads the demand persistence and chooses to lean — that disagreement is the interesting part of the page, not a bug.
