Why this fits

PRISM is Newton's plate. The whole project rests on a 1666 metaphor: white light enters a prism, decomposes into a spectrum, the decomposition is measurable. This direction takes that literally — a Royal Society engraving rebuilt for equities.

Italic ledes and oldstyle figures match the existing Bridgewater research-note voice; the parchment ground reads as institutional rather than fintech. The mark holds at 16px because it was designed for movable type.

Why this fits

The 5 attribution dimensions are a vector, not a metaphor — and this direction treats them that way. The mark is the dispersion fan with the prism removed, like an oscilloscope readout or a beam splitter calibration card.

Graphite ground reads as "instrument I trust to be precise." The amber accent is the warning needle when the model says fade. This direction is the one a quant would have on the wall above their terminal.

Why this fits

PRISM already speaks like a Bridgewater Daily Observation — italic ledes, oldstyle figures, oxblood accent. This direction commits to that voice and gives it a typographic mark: a "P" built from 5 stacked bands, each band one attribution dimension, the bowl made of the dominant ones.

The lightest lift from the existing aesthetic. If we want the brand to feel like a continuation of what's already in styles.css, this is it. The mark is also the page header — wordmark and chart in one glyph.
