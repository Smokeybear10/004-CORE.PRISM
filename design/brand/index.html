§ 01 · The Mark

A P built from five horizontal bands.

The bowl is the dominant attribution; the stem is the residual. The cascade — 44, 40, 36 — is a stepped waterfall, narrow enough to read as type, wide enough to read as a chart. It works on parchment, on navy, in two-tone, in outline. It holds at 16 px because it was drawn to.

One brand, three marks, three jobs.

White light enters the prism and refracts into a spectrum of beams. PRISM borrows that idea for stocks: a single price move is the white light, and the model decomposes it into five colored beams — each one a dimension of cause, each beam's thickness reflecting how much that dimension drove the move.
§ 06 · Color System

Oxford navy is the ink. Oxblood is the alarm.

Six roles, six colors. Navy carries 90 percent of the ink. Oxblood is reserved for accent — the trailing dot in the wordmark, the dimension that drove the move. Brass is the rare highlight, for marks against dark ground. Bone is the secondary surface; rule is the structural line.

§ 07 · Typography

Display in Newsreader. Body in Inter. Tabular in IBM Plex Mono.

A small stack, well-tuned. Newsreader reads like a research note — italics carry voice, the optical sizes hold at hero scale. Inter does the structural work. Plex Mono carries dates, tickers, and citations because tabular numbers have to align.
