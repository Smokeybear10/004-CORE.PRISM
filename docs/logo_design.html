Brand intent

A research-grade financial historian, not a retail product. The mark should look like it could go on the cover of an institutional white paper. Quiet confidence over flashy AI energy. The optical metaphor — one beam in, five bands out — is the only motif we lean on; everything else is restraint.

Reference vibe: The Economist covers, Tiempos type specimens, Bridgewater's wordmark, mid-century scientific instrumentation.
Anti-reference: crypto gradients, neon glows, generic AI geometric meshes, anything that looks like an analytics SaaS dashboard.

Logo concepts

1. The Refraction Recommended primary

Equilateral triangle outlined in primary color, point facing right. A single thin beam enters the left edge; five colored bands of equal weight exit the right vertex, fanning slightly. Bands map top-to-bottom to the five attribution dimensions in palette order.

Why it wins

The mark is the thesis. A judge who has never heard of PRISM can read the logo and infer "single move → five-dimensional decomposition" before you speak. The bands also serve as the legend on every chart in the demo, so the logo and the visualizations share a vocabulary.

Watch out

Pink Floyd association. Mitigate by orienting point-right (suggesting flow / a play button) rather than point-down, and by using the muted palette rather than RGB primaries.

2. The Candlestick Refraction Hero illustration

Same prism geometry, but the input beam is replaced by a candlestick — the universally recognized stock-chart glyph. The narrative is fully explicit: a price move enters the system, exits as five attribution bands.

Best for

Title slide of the demo. Pitch-deck cover. Anywhere the project needs to introduce itself in one image.

Watch out

Too illustrative for a favicon or a tight lock-up. Works as a hero, not as a working mark in product UI.

3. The Chromatic Monogram Favicon / app icon

P P P

A serif "P" with a 4 px chromatic-aberration offset on the cyan and red channels — like a printer's misregister, or actual prism dispersion. Reads as a clean monogram from a distance; reveals the optical metaphor on close inspection.

Best for

Favicon, app icon, square avatars, any place the wordmark won't fit. Scales cleanly to 16×16 by collapsing the offset to 1 px. Prints in single color when needed.

Watch out

The optical reference is subtle — some viewers will read it as a plain "P." Acceptable for a favicon; unacceptable as the only mark.

4. The Spectrum Bar Quiet alternative

Wordmark in serif, with a thin five-band bar set tight beneath the baseline. No iconographic mark at all — the spectrum bar is the only chromatic element. Maximum restraint.

Best for

Email signatures, paper headers, slide footers, anywhere a logo would feel loud. Highest institutional credibility of the five.

Watch out

No standalone mark — cannot be used without the wordmark. Limits app icon and small-format use.

6. The Compact Masthead Lockup Applied · Live

PRISM
Persistence Reasoning over Idiosyncratic Stock Moves

The horizontal compact lockup, used in the live demo masthead at demo/static/index.html. A 4px five-band spectrum ribbon sits at the very top edge of every page, followed by the wordmark with an inline mini-mark and the italic-serif tagline beneath. Reads as one branded element at small heights without needing the triangle icon.

Where it ships

Top sticky masthead of the demo. Spectrum ribbon is page-wide; the lockup itself sits in the masthead-inner container. Right side of the masthead carries the live status and model id, untouched from the original layout.

Why it works at this scale

The triangle icon gets illegible below 32 px. The five-bar inline mark keeps the "five-dimensional system" cue at the same vertical height as the cap-height of PRISM, which the triangle cannot.

5. The Five-Slice Mark Reserve

Five triangular slivers fanning from a single vertex, each colored per the dimension palette. Reads as a badge or a research-paper seal.

Best for

Stamps and seals — the back cover of a printed white paper, a "research" badge, the hex on a sticker.

Watch out

Loses legibility small. Most likely of the five to read as "generic chart icon."

Recommendation

Ship a logo system, not a single mark.

Usage rules

  • Never render the five bands in saturated rainbow. Always the muted palette above.
  • Never rotate the prism off its axis — no perspective, no tilt, no isometric.
  • Wordmark is always all-caps. Never Prism or prism.
  • Dark mode: invert primary and bone. Keep band hexes unchanged — they read on either ground.
  • Minimum clear space around any mark equals the height of the cap "P".
  • Minimum size for the Refraction mark: 32 px tall. Below that, fall back to the Chromatic Monogram.
  • The dimension–color mapping is fixed. Demand is always red. Macro is always indigo. Do not reorder for aesthetic reasons.
  • The 4 px page-spectrum ribbon is required at the top of every product page. It is the brand's most consistent surface — never cover, color-shift, or animate it.
  • In the masthead lockup (#6), the inline 5-bar mark is always at cap-height of the wordmark and always to the right of PRISM with a 14 px gap. Never above, below, or before the wordmark.
