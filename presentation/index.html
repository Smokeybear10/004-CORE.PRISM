An equity price-action tagger — structural or transient?

Srilekha/ Sophia/ Henry/ Thomas
The problem

Stocks move sharply. The market disagrees about why.

Earnings prints. Surprise filings. Sector rotations. Geopolitical shocks. Every day produces moves no one can fully attribute — and the “why” is the difference between a structural break and a buyable overreaction.

Why current tools fall short

Three options — none of them tell you what to do.

01 / 03
Sentiment
“The article reads positive. Score: +0.72.”
→ One scalar. Too coarse to act on.
02 / 03
Headlines
“AMD jumps 11% after AI partnership news.”
→ Reactive narrative. No analytical structure.
03 / 03
Naive LLM
“Investors saw this as validation of AMD’s AI strategy…”
→ Confident paragraphs. No citations. No accountability.

None of them tell you whether the move will persist or revert.

Our approach

A price move is one signal. The text around it has structure.

Demand
Volume, customers, share.
Pricing
Price, mix, discounting.
Competitive
Rivals, moats, entrants.
Mgmt
Guidance, execution, tone.
Macro
Rates, FX, geopolitics.
The decision

From the dimensions, we ask one question.

Structural
LEAN
A real change in the business. Ride the move — the market is right.
vs.
Transient
FADE
Overreaction or noise. Bet on reversion — the market overshot.

Not sentiment. Not a headline. A structural verdict, with citations, that you can defend.
