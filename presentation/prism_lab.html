PRISM — animation lab

Six variants of the prism animation. Tell me which to lock into Section 01.

Variant A

Refract & hold

What it does: The current behavior. Black bar grows in → prism fades in → 5 beams refract out staggered → tags appear. Final state freezes.
Variant B

Refract & loop

What it does: Same composition as A, but cycles forever — fades out, restarts. Good if the slide stays on screen for a while and you want continuous motion.
Variant C

Sequential reveal

What it does: Each beam reveals one at a time with a 600 ms beat between. Each pulses thicker on entry then settles. Use it when narrating — pacing matches "demand … pricing … competitive …" speech.
Variant D

Refract & flatten

What it does: Beams refract at angles (like A), then rotate around their endpoints to land as 5 parallel horizontal lines. Tags appear after the flatten completes. Reads as: "decompose, then index."
Variant E

Burst

What it does: Bar charges in fast (600 ms), prism flashes amber on contact, all 5 beams shoot out simultaneously with a thickness pulse. Punchy / kinetic. Total runtime ~1.8 s. Best for live presentation — it's quick.
Variant F

Wavelength

What it does: Beams render as sine waves at 5 different frequencies — the actual physics of why a prism splits light. Each "factor" has its own wavelength. Most science-y; reads as "different signals refract differently."
